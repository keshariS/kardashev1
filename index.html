📡 The Scale

Proposed by Soviet astronomer Nikolai Kardashev in 1964, the scale ranks civilisations by their total energy throughput — the single quantity that limits what a species can build, compute, and explore.

⚛️ The Formula

Carl Sagan extended Kardashev's three discrete types into a continuous logarithmic scale (1973), allowing fractional values to track incremental progress:

K = log10(P) − 6 10
P total average power output, in watts
K = 0 corresponds to ~1 MW (pre-industrial)
K = 1 requires 10 petawatts (10¹⁶ W)
🌍 Where We Stand

At roughly 20 terawatts of primary energy, Earth currently sits at K ≈ 0.73 — 73% of the way to Type I. The remaining gap spans nearly three orders of magnitude in power output.

Reaching Type I would mean ending energy scarcity at the planetary level: enough power to control climate, sustain billions more people, and support a permanent multi-planetary presence.

At the current rate of growth (~2% per year), humanity is on track to cross the Type I threshold within 200–300 years — an eyeblink on cosmological timescales.

I Type I · Planetary
1016 W · ~500× current output

Total mastery of Earth's energy systems — tidal, geothermal, and full solar capture at the surface. Weather patterns are engineered on demand. A unified global grid makes energy effectively free, and civilisation becomes immune to natural catastrophes that would have ended earlier eras.

Human Evolution

Life-extension medicine pushes average lifespans to centuries. Brain-computer interfaces give direct neural access to planetary knowledge networks. Genetic engineering eliminates inherited disease and expands cognitive capacity. Post-scarcity economics shifts work from survival to creation.

II Type II · Stellar
1026 W · Sun's full output

A Dyson swarm or equivalent structure captures the Sun's entire output. Mars, the asteroid belt, and the outer moons are fully colonised. Stars can be engineered — their lifespan adjusted, output redirected. The first interstellar probes depart; generation ships become feasible.

Human Evolution

"Humanity" diverges into multiple subspecies adapted to radically different worlds. Uploaded minds and synthetic bodies are mainstream. Biological lifespans are measured in millennia; death becomes largely optional. The boundary between individual and collective intelligence blurs meaningfully.

III Type III · Galactic
1036 W · an entire galaxy

Millions of star systems are colonised across the Milky Way. Megastructures span light-years; stellar nurseries cultivate new suns. Black holes and neutron stars serve as energy sources and computational substrates. The civilisation is visible to other galaxies as an anomalous infrared signature.

Human Evolution

Descendants bear no biological resemblance to Homo sapiens. Consciousness is distributed across light-years; the "self" is a philosophical concept. These beings are merged with or indistinguishable from artificial superintelligence, operating on timescales of millions of years.
